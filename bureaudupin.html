Bureau Dupin

January first 2013, the body of Marja Nijholt was found in Oss. Despite extensive efforts the police was not able to bring the investigation to a satisfying conclusion. With Bureau Dupin we have the unique opportunity to attempt to solve this mystery.

The case

Oss, January 1, 2013. On the morning of New Year's Day, a passerby finds the dead body of a woman in the driveway of a home in Oss. It's Marja Nijholt, a 48-year-old woman from Enschede.

Some days before New Year's Eve Marja Nijholt takes the train from her hometown to Gronau, just across the German border, where she stays in a hotel. On New Year's Eve the hotel is called by a man who is looking for Marja. When the hotel owner tells Marja that a call was made, she is mortified and leaves the hotel very panicked. She takes the train back to Enschede, but does not go home. Instead, she travels on to Oss, where she arrives in the afternoon on New Year's Eve.

That afternoon and evening Marja is seen by people in various places in Oss and spotted by cameras. She makes a worried, frightened impression. She tells passers-by that she is looking for a cheap hotel, and that she is fears that she will be murdered. In the evening and at night Marja wanders through Oss towards Berghem and back. She's not seen again until a passer-by walking their dog finds the body of Marja in the morning.

My involvement

In 2013 the police collected all evidence they could find, including data regarding phone use. This data set of about 95.000 records consists of five fields: "Who (1) called whom (2) at what moment in time (3) from what location (4) for what duration (5)?" A team of an intelligence expert, a telecommunications experts and two data scientists was allowed access to this sensitive data set to have a second look at the telecom data.

Initially we focused on understanding the pseudonomyzed data. We identified key players, conducted spatial and social network analyses and looked at remarkable patterns. We also managed to distinguishe between specifically requested data and mast data, which was requested merely due to being in the (spatial) vicinity. However, to fully make use of the wisdom of the crows, I started creating publicly-accessible tools.

The primary tool allows users to filter for IDs by inputting requirements, such as "has over 100 records" or "has had contact with (the known IDs of) Marja". Additionally, IDs can be investigated: a user can for example request to see a map containing all previous locations or the frequency of (incoming and outgoing) records sorted by second party. This tool was a huge hit: in the first month over 10.000 user interactions were recorded. Since I've created and put into production several other tools: one to provide time-based visualizations of IDs activity, one to further focus on spatial patterns and I have been involved in a tool for social network analysis.

Media

Our story has not gone unnoticed. In podcast "De Nieuwjaarsmoord", the nickname that the case had gotten in 2013, the investigation is followed closely. They cover ongoing and new leads in our investigation, mention and discuss any potential breakthroughs and conduct interviews with relatives and people close to Marja. Podcast "Op het bureau" zooms out from the specifics and allows for more general discussion on subjects related to (developments in) the Marja Nijholt case.

In September 2022 a documentary series is planned to release on Videoland, the Dutch RTL-owned competitor of Netflix. Although the series is still in production, we know that Marja's story will be told, leaving plenty of space for the mysterious circumstances of her passing and our efforts in solving the mystery.
