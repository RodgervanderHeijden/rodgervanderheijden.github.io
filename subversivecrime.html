What is subversive crime?

Frankly, subversive crime can both be "nearly everything" and "almost nothing". Common examples include money laundering or production and/or distribution of drugs, but acts like (digital) harassment, tax evasion or matchfixing can also be considered subversive (but aren't necessarily so!). In fact, it's quite well-known that Pablo Escobar used to build houses, football fields and schools to stay in good graces with the (poor) people. On the other hand, a matiral dispute escalating into murder is not seen as subversive.

A cynic could remark that it's impossible to fight something if its definition may include providing housing, opportunities for sports and education while it may exclude murder.

My involvement

Formulating a succesfull integral approach to tackling subversive crime requires all parties to hold a common view on the problem. However, as the many subproblems affect many areas of society, just as many jurisdictions are involved. The RIEC aims to achieve that by intensifying collaboration between partners, by increasing the information position and by developing both preventative and restrictive interventions.

During my (paid) internship at RIEC I looked into the spatial component: a research topic which has been extensively studies in the context of "regular" crimes. Can we find commonalities between the areas where subversive crime manifests itself, and can we explain why these areas are specifically prone to this type of crime?

Results

Presumably to no surprise, I'm not at liberty to discuss the findings in much detail. In a general sense however, I was able to develop models that approximated areas prone to crime in general and subversive crime specifically, showing that the physical surroundings do indeed play a role in incidence of subversive crime. The models also showed clear differences in the factors of 'ideal' areas for both types of crime, indicating that just the characteristics of an area can predict what type of crime is present. Furthermore, presence of subversive crime was shown to be partially explainable to the characteristics of neighbouring areas as well.
