Deforestation

The island of Kalimantan contains a big rainforest. At least, for now it does. In a case of the World Wilflife Fund (WWF) and Deloitte we were given access to a dataset detailing the rainforest in 15 by 15 meter plots of land - which resulted in 325 million data points - which was being used to predict deforestation. Fortunately, the dataset is very, very imbalanced (0.000018% minority class).

We investigated several resampling techniques to better balance the dataset and subsequently trained models to see whether the resampling had any adverse effect. Unfortunately I'm not at liberty to discuss the findings in detail, but with a combination of undersampling the majority class and generating synthetic datapoints of the minority class we achieved no significant loss in predictive performance while speeding up training threefold.

Mobility as a Service

We analyzed how the coverage and availability of public transport in smaller villages impacted the desires inhabitants have of public transport.

Shining a light on the dark web

The dark web is home to marketplaces where criminals can easily sell illicit substances. We scraped one such marketplace, analyzed the data and visualized our findings.

Diffusion of extremism

Polarizing opinions are seldom developed in isolation: one's social network is an important context where extreme opinions can be cultivated. We study whether we can witness and explain why and how radically right opinions spread through a digital network with loose ties.

Chest X-rays: a case against deep learning?

Research into using deep learning architectures in medical contexts has generated a lot of interest of the last years. Well-known master of data science Andrew Ng claimed to have created a model outperforming domain experts, I set out to verify (and improve) his findings.

OddSpot

Sometimes you check your skin and see a new spot developing. You might even say: "what an odd spot". I studied whether a limited set of questions could allow for diagnosing two types of precursors of skin cancer.

Language learning assistant
